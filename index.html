About GlycoPredict AI

GlycoPredict AI is a responsive analytics platform designed to bridge the gap between data science and clinical workflows. By analyzing metabolic biomarkers and demographic variables, the system computes the statistical risk of diabetes onset.

This application utilizes client-side decision algorithms derived from logistic regression modeling trained on the Pima Indians Diabetes Dataset, which compiles health indices of female patients of Pima Indian heritage aged 21 and older.

Mathematical Foundation

Probability (P) = 1 / (1 + e^-z)
z = -8.404 + 0.123*Pregnancies + 0.035*Glucose - 0.013*BP + 0.089*BMI + 0.945*DPF + 0.015*Age

*Skinfold Thickness and Insulin variables represent minimal weights within this specific regression run.

Clinical Variables Glossary

Pregnancies:

Number of times the patient has been pregnant.

Glucose:

Plasma glucose concentration measured two hours after an oral glucose tolerance test (normal level: < 140 mg/dL).

Blood Pressure:

Diastolic blood pressure (mmHg). Ideal diastolic pressure lies between 60 and 80 mmHg.

Skinfold Thickness:

Triceps skin fold thickness (mm), used to estimate body fat percentage.

Insulin:

Two-hour serum insulin level (μU/mL). Serves as a gauge for insulin resistance.

BMI:

Body Mass Index calculated as weight in kilograms divided by height in meters squared (normal range: 18.5 - 24.9 kg/m²).

Diabetes Pedigree Function (DPF):

A score computing diabetes hereditary impact based on family history. Higher values denote stronger genetic risks.

Age:

Patient's age in years, correlating with risk increases over time.
